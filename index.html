Welcome & How to Use This Guide

This guide provides 30 targeted ChatGPT prompts designed to help actuarial students master key concepts for Exams P (Probability), FM (Financial Mathematics), and CS2 (Modelling & Survival Models).

Think of ChatGPT as your AI study partner. Use these prompts to:

  • Deepen Understanding: Get clear explanations of complex topics, often with relatable examples.
  • Practice Application: Work through problems, simulations, and case studies generated by AI.
  • Identify Weaknesses: Use quizzes and targeted questions to find areas needing more focus.
  • Explore Concepts: Ask "what if" questions and explore variations on core ideas.

Each page in this guide features:

  • A specific, ready-to-use Prompt for ChatGPT.
  • A list of relevant Key Topics covered by the prompt.
  • A suggested Use Case explaining *why* and *when* this prompt is helpful.
  • Ideas for Potential Follow-up Questions to ask ChatGPT for deeper exploration.

Bonus Tip: Don't just copy and paste! Modify these prompts. Add details like "explain like I'm 15," ask for Python code, request different scenarios, or specify the desired output format (e.g., "summarize in bullet points"). The more specific you are, the better ChatGPT can assist you.

Good luck with your studies!

Bonus Section
How to Use These Prompts Better: Power-Up Your AI Study Sessions
Maximizing Value:
  • Simplify: Add phrases like `"explain like I'm 15"` or `"explain in simple terms"` to get easier-to-understand answers, especially for complex topics.
  • Guided Learning: Ask ChatGPT to `"give me a hint only"` for a specific problem, try solving it yourself, and then ask for the full solution to check your work.
  • Vary Parameters: Reuse prompts but change the inputs. For example, ask for the bond schedule with a different coupon rate, or explain a distribution using a different real-world scenario.
  • Practical Application: Request examples using specific tools like `"Show me how to simulate this in Python"` or `"Create an example using Excel formulas"` for hands-on learning and practical skill-building.
  • Specify Format: Ask for responses in specific formats like `"Summarize this in bullet points"`, `"Create a comparison table"`, or `"Generate multiple-choice questions"`.
  • Go Deeper: After getting an initial answer, ask follow-up questions like `"Can you elaborate on that assumption?"` or `"What are the limitations of this model?"`
  • Combine Prompts: Try merging ideas from different prompts, e.g., "Simulate the bond amortization schedule from prompt 12 in Python."
